Project details

Helping shoppers understand the brand, browse products, and buy with confidence.

Gataama needed a cleaner, more structured digital experience. The redesign keeps the expressive Afrocentric mood while improving navigation, content hierarchy, product discovery, and checkout confidence.

The brief

Design problem

The existing brand expression was strong, but the site needed clearer pathways for new users to learn the story, compare items, trust the product details, and complete purchase decisions with less friction.

Design process

From audit to final interface, every design decision was tied to user clarity.

01

Audit the current experience, identify weak hierarchy, and map key drop-off moments.

02

Define target shoppers, their motivations, and the proof they need before purchase.

03

Rebuild the information architecture around discovery, product evaluation, and checkout.

04

Create a dark premium UI system with reusable cards, product blocks, forms, and states.

Research notes

User needs

  • Understand product value quickly.
  • See authenticity and cultural context.
  • Compare products without confusion.

Business needs

  • Improve product storytelling.
  • Create stronger conversion paths.
  • Make new sections easier to scale.

Pain points

  • Navigation felt too hidden.
  • Cards did not always explain items.
  • Checkout reassurance came too late.

Opportunities

  • Use clearer section grouping.
  • Add stronger visual hierarchy.
  • Repeat trust signals across flows.

Target audience

Style-conscious shoppers who want products with identity, meaning, and a smooth buying path.

Persona

Persona

The quick buyer

Needs price, size, quality, and delivery details fast.

Persona

The gift buyer

Goals

01

Make the story visible

Introduce Gataama through a stronger editorial hero and cultural product narrative.

02

Improve browsing

Use sharper cards, filters, and product sections so users understand options faster.

03

Clarify checkout

Bring size, shipping, return, and payment reassurance closer to purchase decisions.

04

Scale the system

Create reusable visual patterns for future campaigns, collections, and product pages.
