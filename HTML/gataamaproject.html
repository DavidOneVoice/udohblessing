Overview

From scattered content to a credible product ecosystem.

Gataama needed more than a visual refresh. The public website was hard to navigate, inconsistent across pages, and disconnected from the operational tools the team needed to manage content, events, petitions, country profiles, and community engagement.

I rebuilt the experience around product design fundamentals: clearer information architecture, a more persuasive brand story, reusable UI components, and an admin dashboard that made the platform easier to maintain after launch.

Project goals

Create a digital home that feels modern, trustworthy, and simple to operate.

01

Clarify the story

Restructure the homepage and content pages so visitors quickly understand Gataama's mission, programs, petitions, and impact.

02

Design for action

Make key pathways such as donation, country exploration, blogs, petitions, and conference registration easier to discover.

03

Support internal teams

Create an admin environment for publishing, moderation, analytics, and platform management without engineering dependency.

The problem

The old platform created friction for visitors and administrators.

The experience lacked a cohesive system. Users had to work too hard to understand where they were, what Gataama offered, and how to participate. Admin users also lacked a structured workflow for keeping the platform alive.

  • Navigation labels and page hierarchy did not match user expectations.
  • Important calls to action competed with low-priority content.
  • Visual styles, spacing, and content patterns varied from section to section.
  • Country, blog, petition, and conference flows needed clearer management tools.

Research insights

The redesign centered on user trust, orientation, and confidence.

Insight 01

Visitors needed immediate context.

People should understand the organization's purpose before being asked to donate, sign, read, or register.

Insight 02

Content needed stronger hierarchy.

Programs, countries, articles, and petitions required repeatable patterns so each page felt connected to the same product.

Insight 03

Admins needed a control room, not scattered tools.

The admin dashboard had to bring publishing, moderation, content creation, analytics, and user management into one predictable system.

A scalable platform is not only what visitors see — it is also how confidently internal teams can keep it accurate, current, and useful.
