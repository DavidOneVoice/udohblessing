Case Study · Website Redesign

Gataama Website Redesign: a digital Afrocentric experience

A responsive product design case study for an African-inspired commerce experience — balancing storytelling, cultural identity, conversion clarity, and a premium interface system across web and mobile.

Explore UI System View UX Process

Project details

Designing a clearer path from cultural discovery to confident purchase.

The redesign focuses on product storytelling, information architecture, and an interface language that feels modern without losing Gataama’s rich visual character. Every section was structured to help users understand the brand, browse confidently, and complete key journeys with less friction.

Role

Product Designer

UX research, UI design, responsive systems, interaction strategy.

Timeline

6 Weeks

Discovery, synthesis, wireframes, visual design, and handoff.

Platform

Responsive Web

Desktop-first presentation refined for tablet and mobile commerce flows.

Focus

UX + UI System

Navigation clarity, product hierarchy, brand trust, and checkout readiness.

Problem statement

The previous experience had expressive visuals, but the journey needed stronger structure.

Core challenge

Users needed a faster way to understand what Gataama offers, why the products matter, and which actions to take next. The interface required clearer hierarchy, stronger product context, and more consistent conversion cues.

Design goals

  • Make brand storytelling and commerce actions feel connected.
  • Improve scanning with modular cards, intentional spacing, and clear content sections.
  • Create a reusable design system that scales across product, content, and checkout pages.

Discovery process

Research translated cultural intent into practical product decisions.

The UX process mapped user expectations across discovery, product evaluation, cart review, and post-purchase trust. Findings were converted into content priorities, interface states, and reusable components.

01 Audit

Reviewed navigation, hero communication, product cards, checkout cues, and moments where users might lose context.

02 Users

Defined shopper needs around authenticity, product quality, sizing clarity, delivery reassurance, and simple browsing.

03 Strategy

Reframed the website around three jobs: introduce the story, make products easy to compare, and reduce purchase hesitation.

04 System

Built a dark premium system with energetic accents, editorial typography, responsive modules, and accessible contrast.

User persona

Designing for a style-conscious shopper who values meaning as much as aesthetics.

Motivations

  • Discover products with cultural significance.
  • Understand quality, materials, and styling context quickly.
  • Buy from a brand that feels credible and contemporary.

Pain points

  • Unclear product categories slow browsing.
  • Weak hierarchy makes key actions harder to identify.
  • Missing reassurance can create checkout hesitation.

UX opportunities

  • Use visual storytelling to create emotional connection.
  • Surface product benefits and delivery information earlier.
  • Standardize cards, buttons, badges, and form states.

Information architecture

A modular site structure keeps the brand story visible while supporting faster shopping.

Primary navigation

Home, collections, new arrivals, story, journal, cart, and account were organized around shopper intent instead of internal brand categories.

Content hierarchy

The homepage sequence moves from campaign story to featured products, social proof, editorial content, and checkout reassurance.

Wireframes

Low-fidelity layouts defined rhythm before visual styling was applied.

The art direction pairs Afrocentric warmth with a sleek digital product language.
